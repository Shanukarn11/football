Parent Questions, Answered

Everything You Need to Know Before You Register Your Child

Straight answers for football families. No jargon. No vague promises.

What is IKF and how does it help my child?

IKF (India Khelo Football) is a not-for-profit platform that gives grassroots footballers a structured, merit-based pathway to be evaluated by real scouts. Your child gets a fair, transparent environment where talent — not money or connections — determines their opportunity.

How much does it cost for my child to participate?

IKF trials are free for girls. For boys, a nominal registration fee may apply. IKF will never charge for selection, club placement, or guaranteed advancement. Any request for such payment is fraud.

Is my child safe at IKF trials?

Yes. All IKF trials run in structured, supervised environments with trained staff. Girls have dedicated girls-only trials with women-led environments under Project IKF Naari Shakti.

Does IKF guarantee my child will get into a club?

No. IKF creates the platform and maximum visibility. All final selection decisions are made by clubs based on merit. IKF does not sell selection — anyone who claims otherwise is not representing IKF.

What happens if my child is not selected?

Non-selection is an honest benchmark, not a verdict. Your child is now part of the IKF ecosystem with a clear picture of where they stand. They can return for future editions better prepared — the door stays open.

Where can I read the full process before registering?

The IKF Parent SOP is available at indiakhelofootball.com/parent_sop — it covers every step from registration to evaluation to what happens after. Read it before your child's first trial.
