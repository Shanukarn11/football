IKF Naari Shakti – Meerut Edition

Meerut's First Mobile Campaign for Girls in Football

FOR THE GIRLS. BY THE GIRLS. OF THE GIRLS.

In partnership with Meerut Youth Football Academy, IKF Naari Shakti is bringing a first-of-its-kind mobile football campaign to Meerut with one mission — to create safe, inclusive and empowering opportunities for girls through sport.

Why Naari Shakti?

Nearly half of girls discontinue sports during adolescence due to limited opportunities, safety concerns and a lack of structured programs designed for them. Yet sports remain one of the strongest tools to build confidence, leadership, resilience and lifelong skills.

IKF Naari Shakti aims to bridge this gap and ensure every girl gets the opportunity to play, learn and grow through football.

What is IKF Naari Shakti Meerut?

IKF Naari Shakti Meerut is a 7-day mobile football roadshow that will travel across the city, visiting schools and communities before culminating in a Mega Girls Football Trial.

The Campaign Includes:

  • ⚽ Girls-only football clinics and assessments
  • 🌸 Menstrual health and wellbeing sessions
  • 🎯 IKF Pathway 360 and football awareness sessions
  • 👩 Female coaches and women-led leadership sessions
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Parent engagement and support workshops
  • 🏆 Access to long-term football development opportunities through Meerut Youth Football Academy

The entire initiative is designed to create a safe environment where girls feel confident stepping onto the football field while parents feel assured about their daughters' sporting journey.

After successfully impacting girls across cities including Jaipur, Mathura, Siwan, Ranipet, Pune and Gorakhpur, IKF Naari Shakti now arrives in Meerut to inspire the next generation of footballers and leaders. The initiative has already engaged 444+ girls across India, proving the demand for safe and structured football opportunities for girls at the grassroots level.

Why Schools Should Join

  • Provide access to football pathways and opportunities.
  • Strengthen community engagement and school reputation.
  • Become a torchbearer for girls' participation in sports.

Schools become more than hosts — they become partners in building the future of women's football in India.
